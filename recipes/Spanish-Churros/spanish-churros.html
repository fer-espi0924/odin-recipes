Easy Spanish Churros

Spain's equivalent of doughnuts!

Churros are one of the most popular foods to enjoy during breakfast, snacks, or at any time of the day. They are famous all over the world and for a good reason. They can be eaten as is or sprinkled with sugar or in rich and creamy Spanish hot chocolate

Ingredients

  • Oil for frying
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1 cup white flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • Sugar or honey, to taste, optional

Kitchen Time

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Pour enough oil into a large heavy bottomed frying pan to reach 2 inches. Set the pan over medium-high heat. The optimal frying temperature is 350 F to 375 F.
  3. In a medium saucepan, combine the water, vegetable oil, salt, and sugar.
  4. Cover and bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Add the flour and baking powder to a medium-sized mixing bowl and stir to combine.
  6. Once the water mixture reaches a boil, slowly pour it into the flour mixture while stirring.
  7. Stir constantly with a fork until it is a smooth dough without lumps.
  8. Immediately spoon the dough into a churrera (a large cookie press) or a pastry bag.
  9. Once the oil reaches 350 F or 375 F, carefully squeeze the dough into the hot oil.
  10. Fry until golden brown.
  11. Remove with a slotted spatula or long-handled fork.
  12. Place on a paper towel to drain
  13. Cut the churros into manageable lengths.
  14. Sprinkle with sugar or drizzle with honey, to taste.
  15. Make a chocolate sauce and use it as a dip for these sweet treats. It's absolutely decadent.
